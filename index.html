A tribute to Fran Kirby

Introduction

Fran Kirby, Chelsea's prolific number 14, has cemented herself as one of Chelsea Women's most iconic and lethal players at the club. This tribute to Fran showcases her talent and strength; some of the many reasons as to why she is revered as one of the greats in Women's football. She is a hugely decorated player, winning multiple Women's Super League titles and Women's FA Cup titles. Considered to be an "all-round forward" player, she manages to time and time again prove to be such a threat in the team, scoring a range of decisive and magical goals. Not only is Fran a talented player for club but for country as well, being an key player in England's finals in both the Europeans Championships and World Cups.

Appreciation

One of the reasons why Fran is so respected in the game is because of her triumphant battle with the rare condition pericarditis. The condition is known to cause inflammation of the pericardium -- a fluid filled sac that covers the heart, which in turn meant, she was to be told by medics that she would not be able to play football again. Despite the a grave outlook to her football career, Fran had not only fought the odds to make a full recovery and comeback to football, but playing at a level that saw her recognised as the top performer at the club, becoming Chelsea's all-time goalscorer and playing a crucial part in the many trophies that followed.
